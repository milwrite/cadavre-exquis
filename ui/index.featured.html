Exquisite Corpse

Cadavre Exquis — A Surrealist Parlay

MoMA defines the game: each participant takes turns writing on a sheet of paper, folds it to conceal the contribution, and passes it to the next player. The result is a collective work which, in theory, is free of the limitations we face as individuals.

Here, André Breton’s “pure psychic automatism” becomes a round-robin of one-to-five-word turns between human players and the model. The poem accumulates silently until someone closes it. Then the sequence is revealed, and the model offers a close reading.

“The forms of surrealist language are possibly best suited to dialogue, when two thoughts confront one another, the one reacting to the other.” — André Breton
